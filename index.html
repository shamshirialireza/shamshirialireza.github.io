Publications

Geospatial Machine Learning for Project Financial Decision Support

Built an end-to-end CatBoost-based machine learning model to support financial decision-making for predicting project statuses based on socio-economic and financial factors. One of the core goals was to handle highly imbalanced, sparse datasets with major categorical features, which made it a perfect challenge. The final model achieved 94% accuracy, 91% precision, 98% recall, with training and testing accuracies of 90% and 89% respectively, and an ROC AUC of 84%. To make the results even more actionable, I integrated a geospatial visualization tool to map out prediction probabilities, giving users an intuitive way to interpret results across different regions.
Alireza Shamshiri, Kyeong Rok Ryu, Mohsen Shahandashti & June Young Park
Springer - Innovative Infrastructure Solutions, 2025.

LLMs In-Context Learning for Long-Context Sentiment Analysis

I took on the tricky challenge of testing how well GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5 Pro, and Claude 3.5 Sonnet models can handle sentiment analysis on long and ambivalent opinion-packed documents. It turns out, when people have a lot to say, their opinions can flip back and forth in the same text, which makes it tough for models to keep up. One important part of this project was catching hidden data contamination in the datasets to make sure the results were actually meaningful and reliable. After putting the models through their paces in zero-shot, few-shot, and many-shot scenarios. Finally, an average +21% boost was achieved in handling those long, mixed-feelings opinions.
Alireza Shamshiri, Kyeong Rok Ryu, June Young Park
ArXiv, 2024.

Graph Attention Networks for Relation Extraction and Multi-Label Text Classification

Built a graph attention network to handle long and complex documents. A Bi-LSTM layer was combined with pre-trained GloVe embeddings, boosting its ability to track meaning across sequences. The final setup achieved a solid 78% micro F1 score on multi-label classification tasks. To see how it stacked up, a CNN model was also trained on the same data, and their performances were benchmarked against each other. In the end, all results were visualized and interpreted to better understand how each model handled semantic depth and label dependencies.
Alireza Shamshiri, Salah Chowdhury, Mahdis Hamisi, Kyeong Rok Ryu, Jiseul Kim
Elsevier - Advanced Engineering Informatics (AEI) - Under review, 2025

Enhanced Topic-Sentiment Detection For Public Opinion Analysis

Trained transformer-based classifiers using BERT and RoBERTa models, which pushed sentiment classification accuracy to over 87% for ambivalent sentiment analysis cases. To tackle longer texts, a Bi-LSTM layer was integrated, giving the model the muscle to handle long-context documents without losing track of sentiment shifts. On top of that, a novel unsupervised topic modeling pipeline was developed to uncover overlooked topics and subtopics, consistently hitting top coherence scores. The result was a system that didn’t just detect what people were saying, but also how they felt about it, even when their opinions weren’t so clear-cut.
Alireza Shamshiri, Kyeong Rok Ryu, June Young Park, Mohsen Shahandashti, Joowon Im
Dissertation, 2025.

Streamlining Public Opinion Analysis

Developed a comparative sentiment analysis and thematic pre- and post-temporal analysis framework. The idea was to go beyond just measuring how people felt and instead track how their sentiments and the topics they talked about shifted over time. The project offered a clearer, more intuitive way to capture not just what people were saying, but how those conversations evolved before and after major events.
Alireza Shamshiri, Kyeong Rok Ryu, Steven McCullough, June Young Park
Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) - BuildSys, 2022.
